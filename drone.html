About This Project

For their power, maneuverability, and the mechanical simplicity with which they achieve it, drones have earned a special place in my heart. The consumer drone market however, is dominated by fragile, technology laden and impractical to replace plastic shells, or rugged CNC cut carbon fiber plates. In my approach to designing a drone, I sought the best of both worlds; the mechanical simplicity and ease of assembly of carbon plates, and the versatility of 3D printable plastic parts. The core principle of the design is sandwiching four tubular arms between two plates, and though it can be explained in a single line, there are quite a few benefits from this construction. Not only does the tubular arm profile allow the motor cables to run safely down the arms, it results in a significantly more rigid arm than a flat bar of the same weight. Further, since each arm is attached with four screws, replacing a broken one takes a few minutes, and the four arms together behave as standoffs to which the plates are clamped, resulting in a drone body that is much more rigid than one single plate. Higher rigidity means lower susceptibility to vibrations, which means cleaner flight characteristics.

A crucial step in the assembly is to equip the motors and motor driver board with bullet connectors. This way, if an arm must be replaced no extra soldering is required. In the case of a violent impact where the arm is not just fractured, but flung away from the drone, the connectors will disconnect before the solder pads on the driver board break, preventing permanent damage to the expensive part. (This is a good reason to use connectors even on carbon plate builds)

At the outer ends of the arms the motors are housed in vented cups. The enclosed design mitigates damage to the motors in a crash, as well as the entry of dust and dirt. Meanwhile the vent allow the cooling required by these motors, as at full throttle they dissipate many dozens of watts of heat.

An afterthought: A long armed prototype with significantly smaller, lower RPM motors spinning larger, low pitch, two blade propellers, with the goal of improving flight time and range by increasing the drone's efficiency. Despite its wingspan, it is weaker and more sluggish the original build, but does indeed live up to its purpose.
